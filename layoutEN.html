Editorial design is a specialty of graphic design that works with the layout of books, ebooks, photobooks, newspapers, magazines, and catalogs. These materials have texts, photos, illustrations and infographics, so the distribution of visual elements must be objective, harmonic and comfortable for reading.

To maintain uniformity, have reference points, balance, and center the elements in the layout, we use a grid, where it is possible to have this symmetry, serving as a basis for the rational distribution of the visual weights of the page elements . A grid is composed by dividing the page dimensions vertically and horizontally, forming frames that will serve as a basis for placing the texts and images of the publication. These divisions respect the top, right, bottom and left margins of the page, delimiting the workspace or smudge area. Opposite to what one might imagine, the use of grids in diagramming does not limit creativity. It allows the view of the finished page to have balance and reading fluidity.

To choose the typography, we took into account the style of the publication. Through the graphic design of the editorial material, we guide the designer in the selection of font families, styles (regular, bold or italic) and lateral expansion (normal, condensed or expanded).

All images used in the production of editorial material are treated and technically adjusted for printing, thus making them more attractive to the reader.

If the choice of printing is colored, we also study the colors that will be used. Colors communicate specific sensations, so we put them in line with the main objective of the graphic project.

The choice of paper is essential for the good final result. Very white and glossy papers make reading uncomfortable when the texts are in large quantity, but enhance the colors and images. They are more suitable for art books or photobooks due to the appreciation of the images. For text books, the ideal is to use off-white papers that have a yellowish color and therefore do not cause fatigue to the reader at the time of reading. In addition, smaller weights make the publication lighter, another item that should be considered for publications with a large number of pages. Respecting the interaction that the reader has with the publication and using all available resources, it is possible to develop creative editorial projects and have an excellent final result.
